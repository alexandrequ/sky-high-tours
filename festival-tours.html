02 — Festival

Festival Tours

Dance-dramas in monastery courtyards — sacred masks, colossal trumpets, and centuries of ritual, still performed exactly as they always have been.

Festivals in Ladakh take the form of dance-dramas in the courtyards of monasteries. Monks wearing colourful robes and masks perform cham — sacred mask dances that represent the purification of mind, and also the triumph of good over evil.

The monks also play traditional instruments: drums, conch shells, cymbals, and colossal trumpets called dungchen, whose low tone rolls across the monastery courtyard and out into the valley.

Each monastery holds its own festival on dates set by the Tibetan lunar calendar, so they shift from year to year. We'll help you time your trip to catch one — or several — depending on your itinerary.
