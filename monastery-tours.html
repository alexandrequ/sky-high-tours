01 — Culture

Monastery Tours

More than 35 Tibetan Buddhist monasteries preserve Ladakh's rich cultural and architectural heritage — a living refuge for a tradition repressed elsewhere.

Buddhism is thought to have been known in Ladakh in the 7th century, when the area was under the control of Tibet. In modern times, since China invaded Tibet and repressed the practice and observance of Buddhism, Ladakh has become a refuge where Tibetan Buddhism can flourish.

Ladakh has more than 35 Tibetan Buddhist monasteries that preserve the rich cultural and architectural heritage of the region. The monasteries host most of the festivals that feature elaborate dance-dramas, where lamas in colourful robes and masks perform mimes representing different aspects of Buddhism — the progress of the soul toward enlightenment, and the triumph of good over evil.

Many monasteries and sights are within easy reach of Leh. You can take a day trip, or stay over night to go deeper — Likir, Thiksey, Lamayuru, Diskit and Spituk are among the most rewarding. We'll help you plan your visit to coincide with the many festivals held around Ladakh throughout the year.
